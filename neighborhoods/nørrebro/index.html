nørrebro

Nørrebro is a bit of a hipster neighborhood that has experienced gentrification over the last two decades–and both the bad and good that comes with it. It’s the neighborhood with the most activity, from hipsters and immigrants alike.

Breakfast/Brunch

Møller Coffee and Kitchen ✔️, rv

A great small dish (for sharing) brunch place. Coffee is decent, but 💰ensive. Recommended to grab coffee at one of the other recommended spots before your reservation.

Brus ✔️

Brus is a mostly a trendy beer hangout, but they do a decent brunch on the weekends.

Ali’s ✔️

Ali’s is definitely a no-frills, less “brunchy” kind of stop, but it is a good example of the mix of cultures in Nørrebro. Just know that it doesn’t look like that much on the inside.

Grød ✔️

A simple breakfast spot with a simple purpose: delicious porridge. They do oatmeal, but they also use a number of grains, which included sweet and savory. There are three Grød locations Copenhagen (inner city and Vesterbro).

Good for a quick and tasty stop.

Mirabelle rv

Mirabelle is located alongside of Bæst (pizz) and Brus (Beer and Food). It is a bit for French in style, with a large focus on bread.

Sidecar rv

A simple and classic (by Danish standards) brunch spot.

Heaps Good Cafe

A little of the beaten path, Heaps is another classic and

Wulff & Konstali

Don’t have too much to say about this one, other than it’s good if you’re on the side of Nørrebro closer to the city center.

Coffee

Coffee Collective ✔️

Like all great coffee spots, Coffee Collective is expensive, good, and hipster. There are three locations in Copenhagen–this one, Vesterbro, and City Center

Andersen & Maillard ✔️

A relatively new addition to Nørrebro, Andersen & Maillard is quite similar to Coffee Collective, but you can buy more breakfast items and pastries.

Lunch

Durum Symfoni ✔️

One of my favorite falafel/kebab places in Copenhagen. Falafel and durum are both made to order.

Jagger ✔️

A decent burger place, not much else to say.

Ramen To Biiru ✔️

A decent ramen place that works in collaboration with Mikkeller, a (relatively) famous Danish brewery.

Ali’s Bakery ✔️

Not to be confused with the Ali’s in the brunch section, this is a no-frills lunch place further into Nørrebro. Cheap and easy, this one is more for the experience of Denmark’s version of multiculturalism.

Smag ✔️

Quick, easy, and healthy salad place. Has several options throughout cph.

La Neta ✔️

Similar to Ramen To Biiru, this is a Mexican restuarant in collaboration with Mikkeller. It is cheap, good, and easily accessible.

Blue Taco ✔️

A decent taco place on Blågårdsgade.

Hooked ✔️

A street food restaurant with good fish options.

Dinner

Cichetti ✔️, rv

An Italian tapas place on Nørrebrogade.

Ma’ed Ethiopian ✔️

Could also be a good lunch option, Ma’ed is simple and delicious. It has a similar vibe as Ali’s Bakery, and is a frequent spot for the vagrant Nørrebro type.

Relæ 💰, rv, *, ✔️

A new Nordic restaurant from one of the chef’s from Noma. A great multi-course dinner experience.

Kiin Kiin 💰, rv, *

A Thai Michelin-star restaurant of which I have only heard good things, and they have a lunch menu in addition to their more expensive dinner menu.

Bæst 💰, rv

Pizza, Danish style, which basically means designer pizza. They have only the best ingredients and dough, and one should expect to pay the price.

Manfreds 💰, rv

A good lunch or dinner spot, if one doesn’t want to pay a Relæ price tag.

Cocktails/Beer/Wine

Brus ✔️

Mentioned before in the brunch section, but this is a popular beer place with around 40 beers on tap–their own and [To Øl beers(https://toolbeer.dk/).

Paloma ✔️

Paloma is close to Brus, but not as crowded–mostly because it’s not as hip. It calls itself a Vermouth bar, so if you’re into that, it’s a great place. They also have a few beers on tap.

Ølsnedkeren ✔️

Good beers at good price. I like Ølsnedkeren because it is more laid back than, say, Brus, and the prices won’t leave as gaping of a hole in your bank account.

The Barking Dog ✔️, rv

A decent cocktails place close to the lakes.

Kølsters Tolv Haner ✔️

A cute beer spot with a great atmosphere inside. I like Tolv Haner for the same reasons as Ølsnedkeren.

Gilt ✔️, rv, 💰

Gilt is a relatively fancy cocktail bar. Their drinks are great.

Sabotøren ✔️

Another place that is tucked away in Nørrebro. They have great wine. It’s also a good idea to check out Restaraunt Omar which is located right next door.

Mikkeller & Friends ✔️

Similar to Brus, Mikkeller is a trendy beer price where you’ll pay hipster prices. Beer is good–and expensive.

Things To Do

Hang out at the Red Square

While not as popular as in years passed, Nørrebro’s Red Square is still quite active, especially on the weekend. When the sun is shining, it’s great to hangout all along the “Grønne Sti”, a walkway and cycle path throughout Nørrebro.

Stroll through Assisten’s Cemetery

A burial place for many famous Danes, including HC Andersen, Søren Kirkegård, and Niels Bohr, Assisten’s is a great example of the Danish utilization of cemetary’s–that is, mostly as a park and place to sit on blanket in the sun with some friends.

Jægersborggade

Super hip street. Has Michelin star restuarants, vintage clothes shops, and a cactus boutique. A definite must on a Saturday.

Sankt Hans Torv & Blågårdsgade

Sankt Hans Torv and Blågårdsgade are great places to hang out, whether it’s a weekend evening or morning. Like many places in Nørrebro, they are a great place to take a stroll down, where you can stop in at a clothes boutique, grab a coffee, or stop for a beer or cocktail.

The Lakes

Like all the other TODOs above, the lakes are a great place to grab a coffee and take a stroll. One can see a variety of people groups around the lakes, from those that live free to those that live the 9 to 4 (thanks to Denmark’s standard 37.5 hour work week).
